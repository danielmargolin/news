The 46th President of the United States. Joe Biden, born on November 20, 1942, has had a long and distinguished career in American politics. He served as a U.S. Senator from Delaware from 1973 to 2009 and as Vice President under President Barack Obama from 2009 to 2017. In 2020, Biden won the presidential election, defeating the incumbent President Donald Trump. Throughout his career, Biden has been known for his moderate political positions and his focus on issues such as healthcare, climate change, and foreign policy. As president, he has prioritized tackling the COVID-19 pandemic, implementing economic recovery measures, and addressing climate change. Biden's presidency has been marked by efforts to bridge political divides and restore a sense of unity in the country.
